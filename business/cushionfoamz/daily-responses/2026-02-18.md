### Template A: General Cushion Help
```
Hey! For outdoor cushions that actually last, density matters more than thickness. 

Go with 2.5lb+ density foam (not the 1.2lb stuff at big box stores). It won't turn into a pancake after one season. 

Also — Sunbrella fabric is worth the extra $ if you can swing it. Water-resistant ≠ waterproof, and Sunbrella handles both better than most.

If you're looking for custom cuts, I found a local place that ships nationwide and their prices beat Amazon for anything custom. DM me if you want the link!
```

### Template B: Specific Foam Recommendation
```
Quick foam breakdown:
- **Indoor seating:** 1.8lb density, medium firm
- **Outdoor seating:** 2.5lb+ density, medium-firm to firm  
- **Dining chairs:** 2.0lb, firm (holds shape under weight)

Thickness depends on use — 3" for dining, 4-5" for lounging.

Pro tip: Get it cut slightly larger than your cover (1/4" all around) so it fills out nice and tight.

Happy to share where I got mine cut if helpful!
```
