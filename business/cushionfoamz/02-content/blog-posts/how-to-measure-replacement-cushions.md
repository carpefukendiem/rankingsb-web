## Why Measurement Matters

Most furniture owners underestimate cushion measurement by ¼ to ½ inch. That doesn't sound like much until you try stuffing a too-large foam block into a cover that won't zip, or sit on a cushion that slides around because it's too small.

**Common measurement mistakes:**
- Measuring the old foam (compressed and misshapen) instead of the cover
- Not accounting for seam allowances inside the cover
- Forgetting that foam needs to be slightly larger than the finished cushion size
- Ignoring thickness variations across different foam densities

The good news? Measuring correctly takes about 10 minutes and requires only a tape measure and flat surface.

## What You'll Need

Before you start:
- **Tape measure** (cloth measuring tape preferred, but rigid tape works)
- **Flat surface** (floor, table, or counter)
- **Your cushion cover** (empty, not stuffed)
- **Notepad** or phone to record measurements
- **Calculator** (for foam density/depth calculations)

---

## Step-by-Step Measurement Guide

### Step 1: Empty the Cover Completely

Remove all old foam, batting, and debris from your cushion cover. Zip or button it closed—this is how you'll measure.

**Why empty?** Old foam compresses unevenly. Measuring compressed foam gives you dimensions for compressed foam, not the cover's actual capacity.

### Step 2: Measure Width (Left to Right)

Lay the empty cover flat on your surface, zipper/button side down.

- Measure from the left seam to the right seam
- Measure at the **middle** of the cushion (not the edges where fabric bunches)
- Record this as your **Width**

**Pro tip:** Measure twice. If you get different numbers, use the larger measurement.

### Step 3: Measure Depth (Front to Back)

- Measure from the front seam to the back seam
- Again, measure at the center of the cushion
- Record this as your **Depth**

### Step 4: Measure Thickness (Height)

This is where most people go wrong.

**Do NOT measure your old foam.** Instead:

1. Lay the empty cover flat
2. Measure from the table surface to the top of the cover
3. This gives you the **cover's capacity**—how thick the foam can be

**Important:** If your cover has a gusset (vertical strip connecting top and bottom panels), measure that gusset height. That's your target thickness.

### Step 5: Account for Foam Compression

Here's the critical part most guides miss:

**Foam should be ordered ½ to 1 inch larger than your cover dimensions.**

Why? Foam compresses when you sit on it. If you order foam exactly matching your cover dimensions, you'll have a saggy cushion after a few weeks of use.

**The Compression Formula:**
- **Width:** Add ½ inch
- **Depth:** Add ½ inch  
- **Thickness:** Order exactly what you measured (or ¼ inch more for high-traffic seating)

## Understanding Foam Density

Now that you have dimensions, you need to choose foam density. This affects comfort, longevity, and price.

### Indoor Foam Options

**Medium Density (1.8 lb/ft³)**
- Best for: Decorative cushions, light use seating
- Lifespan: 3-5 years
- Feel: Soft to medium support
- Price: $

**High Density (2.5 lb/ft³)** — Most Popular
- Best for: Daily-use sofas, dining chairs, window seats
- Lifespan: 7-10 years
- Feel: Medium-firm support, holds shape
- Price: $$

**Luxury/High Resilience (2.8+ lb/ft³)**
- Best for: Heavy-use seating, commercial applications
- Lifespan: 10-15 years
- Feel: Firm support, excellent recovery
- Price: $$$

### Outdoor Foam Requirements

Outdoor cushions need completely different foam:

**Quick-Dry Open-Cell Foam**
- Drains water instead of absorbing it
- Resists mold and mildew
- Essential for pool/patio furniture
- Lifespan: 5-7 years with proper care

**Closed-Cell Marine Foam**
- Completely waterproof
- Best for boat cushions
- More expensive but indestructible
- Lifespan: 10+ years

## Special Cushion Types

### T-Cushions (Box Cushions with Front Extensions)

These have a "T" shape with extra width at the front.

**Measurement method:**
1. Measure the main rectangle (back portion)
2. Measure the width of the front extension separately
3. Note the depth where the extension starts

When ordering, describe it as: "T-cushion, main section [W x D x T], front extension [W x D] starting [X] inches from front"

### Bench Cushions (Single Long Cushion)

For window seats or bench seating:

- Measure the exact length you need
- Consider ordering in **sections** if longer than 48 inches
- Sections are easier to handle, ship, and replace individually

### Chaise Lounge Cushions

These are tricky because they have multiple sections.

**Best approach:** Order as separate cushions for each section (headrest, seat, leg rest) rather than one continuous piece. More comfortable, easier to replace, better fit.

## Measuring for Different Furniture Types

### Couch/Sofa Cushions

Standard sizes don't exist. Even couches from the same manufacturer vary by model year.

**Always measure each cushion individually.** Don't assume the left cushion matches the right. We've seen 2-inch differences on the same sofa.

### Dining Chair Cushions

Most dining chair cushions are:
- Width: 16-20 inches
- Depth: 16-18 inches
- Thickness: 2-3 inches

But measure yours—the difference between a comfortable chair and one that pinches your thighs is often just ½ inch.

### Window Seat Cushions

Window seats are rarely perfectly rectangular. Measure:
- Width at front
- Width at back
- Depth at left
- Depth at right

If your window seat is trapezoidal or has angled corners, contact us for a custom template. We can work with paper patterns.

### Boat/Marine Cushions

Boat cushions face unique challenges:
- Moisture and salt air
- UV exposure
- Vibration and movement

**Always use marine-grade foam.** Measure while covers are dry—wet marine canvas shrinks slightly.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### "My foam is too big for the cover"

You probably measured the old compressed foam instead of the empty cover. Contact us—we may be able to trim it, or you may need to reorder.

### "My cushion is saggy after two weeks"

Foam was undersized or low-density. Next time, add ½ inch to your dimensions and consider higher density foam.

### "The corners won't fill out"

Your foam may be square-cut when your cover has rounded corners, or your foam is under-sized. Try working the foam into corners more aggressively, or contact us about rounded-corner cutting.

### "My outdoor cushions got waterlogged"

You need quick-dry or closed-cell foam, not standard indoor foam. Unfortunately, this requires reordering with the correct foam type.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I measure my old foam instead of the cover?**  
A: Only if the foam is in perfect condition. Compressed, sagging foam gives you compressed, sagging dimensions. Always measure the cover if possible.

**Q: What if my cushion cover is stretchy/spandex?**  
A: Measure it unstretched. Stretchy covers are forgiving, but you still want foam that's slightly larger than the cover's relaxed state.

**Q: How thick should sofa cushions be?**  
A: Standard is 4-6 inches for seat cushions, 2-3 inches for back cushions. But measure your covers—thickness preference varies by furniture style.

**Q: Can you make foam for oddly shaped cushions?**  
A: Yes. For complex shapes, mail us a paper template or the old foam piece itself. We'll match it exactly.

**Q: What's the difference between firmness and density?**  
A: Density = weight/quality of foam (durability). Firmness = how hard/soft it feels. You can have high-density soft foam or low-density firm foam. We typically recommend high-density medium-firm for most applications.

**Q: Do I need batting/wrap around the foam?**  
A: Dacron batting creates a softer crown and smoother surface. Recommended for luxury seating, optional for casual use.
